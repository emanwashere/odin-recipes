Sharwarma Recipe

Description


Shawarma is a classic Middle Eastern street food made with marinated meat (commonly chicken, beef, or lamb) cooked until tender and slightly charred, then wrapped in warm pita bread with fresh vegetables and creamy sauces. Traditionally cooked on a vertical rotisserie, it can be recreated at home using an oven or stovetop pan with the same aromatic spices

ingredients

  • 1 kg boneless chicken thighs or breasts
  • 3 tbsp plain yogurt
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
  • Salt & black pepper to taste

Prep

  1. Mix yogurt, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and all spices in a bowl.
  2. Coat chicken thoroughly in the marinade.
  3. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours (overnight for best flavor).
  4. Preheat oven to 200°C (400°F) or heat a grill/pan.
  5. Roast or grill chicken until cooked through and slightly charred at the edges (about 20–25 minutes).
  6. Slice chicken into thin strips once cooked.
  7. Warm pita bread.
  8. Layer sliced chicken, fresh vegetables, and pickles.
  9. Drizzle with garlic sauce or tahini.
  10. Roll tightly into a wrap and serve hot.
